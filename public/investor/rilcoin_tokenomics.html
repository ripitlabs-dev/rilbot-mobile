Token Utility — What RIL Does
⚡

Pay for Agent Runs

Any agent run on RILBOT can be paid with RIL tokens. Token payments receive a 15% discount versus fiat pricing, driving organic token demand proportional to platform usage growth.

🔒

Stake for Enterprise Access

Users who stake a minimum RIL threshold unlock Enterprise tier features — unlimited runs, priority routing, advanced memory, and API access — without the monthly subscription fee.

🗳️

Governance Voting

RIL holders vote on platform governance proposals: new agent verticals to build, pricing changes, treasury deployments, and protocol upgrades. Token = voice in platform direction.

💰

Revenue Sharing (Planned)

Long-term stakers (12+ month lock) will participate in a revenue sharing pool funded by a percentage of platform gross revenue. Rewards loyalty and reduces circulating supply.

Why RIL Appreciates — The Demand Mechanics
01
Fixed Supply Creates Scarcity. There will never be more than 1,000,000,000 RIL. No governance mechanism, no team decision, no smart contract function can mint additional tokens. As demand grows, price appreciation is the only equilibrium.
02
Agent Runs Create Deflationary Pressure. Every agent run paid in RIL is either burned or sent to the treasury (governance-controlled). Consistent daily agent run volume creates consistent token removal from circulation. More users = more burns = less supply.
03
More Users → More Token Demand. As RILBOT user count grows — from 1,200 in Y1 to 35,000 in Y3 — the aggregate demand for RIL tokens (for payments, staking, and governance) scales proportionally. Token demand is directly coupled to product-market growth.
04
DEX Liquidity Bootstrapped from Public Allocation. 300M RIL (30% of supply) is allocated for DEX liquidity pools at launch. This creates deep, stable trading markets immediately, preventing early volatility that deters institutional holders and long-term stakers.

Risk Disclosures

⚠️ Risk Factors — Please Read Carefully

  • Regulatory Risk: Utility tokens remain subject to evolving regulatory frameworks in the United States and globally. Future SEC, CFTC, or international regulatory actions could affect token usability, transferability, or listing status.
  • Market Risk: Cryptocurrency markets are highly volatile. The value of RIL may decline substantially, including to zero. Past performance of similar tokens does not predict future results.
  • Liquidity Risk: Early-stage DEX markets may have limited depth. Large trades may experience significant price impact. Initial liquidity is not guaranteed to persist.
  • Technology Risk: Smart contract bugs, exploits, or Base network outages could affect token operations. All contracts will be audited prior to launch, but no audit eliminates all risk.
  • Platform Risk: The value of RIL is tied to the success of the RILBOT platform. If RILBOT fails to achieve adoption, token utility and demand may decline.
  • Governance Risk: Token governance mechanisms may produce decisions that negatively affect some token holders. Majority token holders may vote against minority interests.
  • Vesting Risk: Team and treasury token unlock schedules create future sell pressure that may temporarily depress token prices.
