Product & Business
01 What exactly does Rip It Labs do?
⌄
Rip It Labs builds and operates RILBOT — a marketplace of 202 specialized AI agents organized into 36 teams across 30 industries, from legal and healthcare to construction and hotel management. Users subscribe for $49/month and get unlimited access to every agent. Instead of getting a generic chatbot answer, users submit a query to a team of 5–7 specialists that analyze the problem in parallel and deliver a synthesized, structured action plan. Think of it as hiring a full executive team — corporate attorney, financial advisor, HR director, operations manager — available instantly, 24/7, for less than a Netflix subscription.
02 Who is the target customer?
⌄
The core customer is the American SMB owner — the restaurant operator, the solo contractor, the independent real estate agent, the small law firm, the medical practice — anyone who needs expert guidance but can't afford to retain specialists across every domain. There are 33 million small businesses in the US alone. Our secondary customer is the developer building AI-powered products who wants to integrate domain expertise via API rather than build it from scratch. Our tertiary channel is enterprise white-labeling — a healthcare SaaS or legal platform that wants to embed our agent teams into their product under their brand.
03 What's your current traction?
⌄
We are in active development with the platform deployed and functional. Traction metrics — users, revenue, agent runs — are tracked in our live traction dashboard and updated weekly. The honest answer at this stage is that we are pre-revenue and focused on product completion and early distribution. The seed round is being raised to fund the first 18 months of growth: product hardening, GTM, and the first 1,000 paying customers. Investors who want the most current numbers should ask Tim directly on the call — he will always share the real number, not a polished one.
RILCOIN & Token
04 Hot How does RILCOIN work — is it a security?
⌄
RILCOIN is a utility token that powers transactions within the RILBOT ecosystem — users purchase RILCOIN to access premium agents, high-volume API runs, and enterprise features. It is not issued as an investment vehicle and is structured as a utility token under current SEC guidance (similar to how airline miles or in-app currency functions). We are not offering RILCOIN as a security in this raise. That said, we are engaging legal counsel for a formal token opinion letter before any public DEX listing. Investors asking about RILCOIN as part of their investment thesis should understand that its value proposition is tied to platform utility growth, not speculation.
Competition & Moat
05 What's your competitive moat?
⌄
The moat is architectural depth combined with breadth. We have 202 domain-scoped agents with institutional knowledge baked into each one — that's not something you build in a weekend. We have team routing logic that coordinates multi-agent parallel analysis. We have a no-code agent builder that lets any user create a custom specialist. We have RILCOIN as a payment and loyalty layer. And we have a marketplace with discovery, ratings, and cross-vertical network effects. Any single one of those could be replicated by a well-funded team. Building all five simultaneously, in 30 industries, while maintaining coherence across the platform — that's the moat. Time-to-replication at this depth is 2–3 years minimum.
06 Hot What happens when OpenAI / Anthropic builds this?
⌄
Anthropic makes the engine. We built the car. Foundation model companies are infrastructure businesses — they sell capability to developers and enterprises. Their customers are companies like us, not end users who need a Hotel General Manager or a construction compliance specialist. The analogy is Salesforce: AWS didn't kill Salesforce by offering compute. They became Salesforce's biggest vendor. We expect the same dynamic — as AI models get better, our agents get better automatically, and our domain architecture becomes more valuable, not less. The institutional knowledge, the vertical scoping, the team architecture, the marketplace, and the RILCOIN ecosystem are durable independent of which model sits underneath.
Financials & Business Model
07 How do you make money?
⌄
Four revenue streams. Primary: SaaS subscriptions at $49/month (consumer) and $199/month (professional). Secondary: RILCOIN token sales for premium access and enterprise usage tiers. Third: API licensing to developers and white-label partners at volume-based pricing. Fourth: future enterprise contracts with platforms that embed our agent teams. The SaaS subscription is the primary focus for the first 18 months — it's predictable, high-margin, and scalable. The API and enterprise channels open up at scale and provide an expansion revenue layer without requiring proportional headcount growth.
08 What are your gross margins?
⌄
SaaS AI businesses typically target 60–75% gross margins, and we are building toward that range. Our primary COGS is API inference cost (Anthropic Claude), hosting infrastructure, and payment processing. At current model pricing, a typical agent run costs fractions of a cent. A $49/month subscriber generating 500 queries per month still produces strong contribution margins. As we scale volume, we expect to negotiate enterprise API pricing with Anthropic that improves margins further. The no-code builder and API channels have even higher margins since the marginal cost of an additional API key is essentially zero.
09 What are your CAC and LTV numbers?
⌄
We are pre-revenue, so our CAC and LTV numbers are modeled rather than empirical at this stage. Our model assumes: blended CAC of $30–$80 through content marketing, SEO, and product-led growth (PLG) — well below paid channel benchmarks. LTV based on $49/month with 18-month average retention = $882 gross, ~$600 net. That's a 7:1 LTV:CAC ratio at the midpoint — well above the 3:1 SaaS benchmark. As we collect real retention data post-launch, we will update these numbers in our traction dashboard. The first 90 days of paying customer data will be the most important signal we collect.

18 months of runway structured around four priorities: (1) Product hardening — security audit, HIPAA compliance pathway, API stability for enterprise sales; (2) GTM — first marketing hire, content engine, Product Hunt launch, partnership development; (3) Engineering scale — one additional full-stack developer to accelerate the no-code builder and developer API; (4) Legal and entity structure — Arizona LLC, RILCOIN token legal opinion, standard IP assignments. The milestone at month 18 is $50K MRR with 1,000+ paying customers — a Series A data package that commands a $10M–$15M pre-money valuation.
11 When do you break even?
⌄
At a lean burn rate of $35K–$45K/month (2 engineers, 1 marketer, infrastructure, API costs), break-even is at approximately 800–1,000 paying subscribers at the $49/month tier — or a mix of lower-count subscribers with some enterprise contracts. Based on our GTM timeline, we model break-even at month 14–16 after the seed close. We are intentionally building with a small team and AI-assisted operations to keep the break-even threshold low — this is not a business that needs 50 employees to reach profitability.

Team & Operations
13 How many employees do you have?
⌄
Rip It Labs is currently founder-led with a lean core team and contractors supporting specific workstreams. This is intentional — the product was built with AI-assisted development, which allowed us to compress what typically takes a 10-person team into a 2-person operation. The seed round includes headcount expansion: one senior engineer, one growth/marketing hire, and advisory board formation across legal, healthcare, and Web3 verticals. We are not trying to build a big company fast — we are trying to build a profitable, defensible product first, then scale the team proportional to revenue.
14 Key Is Tim the only technical person?
⌄
Tim Cavin is the founding engineer and product architect — he designed and built the RILBOT platform, the agent framework, the RILCOIN blockchain integration, and the full-stack infrastructure. That concentration is a real early-stage risk, and we acknowledge it openly. The mitigation plan is: (1) documentation of all architecture decisions and system designs, (2) code repository organized for contributor onboarding, (3) use of seed capital to hire a second senior engineer in Q1 post-close, and (4) advisor relationships with technical operators who can provide oversight. Concentration risk is common at seed stage — the goal is to reduce it before Series A.
Risk & Compliance
15 What's the biggest risk to this business?
⌄
Honest answer: distribution. The product works. The architecture is sound. The market exists. The biggest risk is not building enough awareness to acquire the first 1,000 customers before the runway runs out. This is why $400K of the seed round is allocated to GTM — not engineering. The second-biggest risk is founder concentration (addressed above). The third is regulatory uncertainty around AI outputs in licensed professions (legal, medical, financial). We manage that risk through scoped disclaimers, required human escalation triggers, and a positioning that is explicitly "advisory guidance," not "licensed professional services."
16 How do you handle AI hallucinations and reliability?
⌄
Every agent has a system-level prompt that includes: prohibited output categories, required disclaimers for regulated-domain responses, and explicit instructions to recommend professional consultation when the query exceeds advisory scope. We run on Anthropic Claude, which uses Constitutional AI training specifically designed to reduce harmful or false outputs. We also log all agent runs for quality review, and our roadmap includes a user feedback loop (thumbs up/down per response) that feeds into agent prompt refinement. No AI is hallucination-free, but the combination of scoped expertise, platform guardrails, and human escalation signals makes RILBOT reliably useful while staying honest about its limitations.
17 What's the HIPAA / compliance situation?
⌄
The general-access tier of RILBOT does not handle PHI (Protected Health Information) and is not marketed as HIPAA-compliant. Healthcare agents provide general wellness and administrative guidance — not clinical decision support. A HIPAA-compliant Healthcare Enterprise tier is on the roadmap for Q3 2026, contingent on: BAA agreements with infrastructure providers (Anthropic, AWS), encrypted PHI storage, audit logging, and a formal HIPAA risk assessment. We will not sell into clinical settings or handle PHI until that infrastructure is in place. This is a conservative approach that protects the company and users while leaving the enterprise healthcare market as an unlocked growth lever.
Deal Terms & Cap Table
18 Who are your current investors?
⌄
Rip It Labs is currently founder-funded. Tim Cavin has self-funded the development of RILBOT through the build phase and is raising the seed round as the first external capital. This means investors in the seed round are getting in at the earliest possible stage — pre-revenue, pre-external-capital, with the most favorable valuation and the most equity leverage. It also means there are no competing investor interests to navigate, no conflicting board preferences, and a clean cap table structure to build from.
19 What's the valuation basis?
⌄
We are raising on a SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) with a $5M–$8M post-money valuation cap. The basis for that cap: comparable pre-revenue AI SaaS companies with working products and clear GTM are raising at $4M–$12M caps in 2025–2026. Our differentiation is architectural depth (202 agents, 36 teams, RILCOIN, no-code builder) — more built than most pre-seed companies, which supports the upper end of the range. We are not anchoring to a DCF — this is a venture bet on a large market with strong founder execution, and the SAFE structure protects investors with a cap while giving the company flexibility.
20 What does the cap table look like?
⌄
Current cap table is 100% founder-owned. Post-seed (assuming $1M raised on a $6M post-money SAFE cap): approximately 83% founder, 17% seed investors, before option pool creation. Series A will require a 10%–15% option pool expansion and the SAFE will convert at that valuation. The clean cap table is one of our genuine advantages at this stage — no messy prior rounds, no conflicting preferences, no convertible notes with complicated terms. Investors coming in now get a simple, clean structure.
